Why dips are normal

Markets move in cycles. Over a long horizon, pullbacks of 5–10% are common — and historically they've recovered. Reacting to a dip is what turns a temporary fall into a permanent loss.

Three things worth remembering

  • Dips are frequent. In a typical year a portfolio sees a double-digit fall at some point — yet most years still finish higher.
  • Recovery is the pattern. Pullbacks of this size have historically recovered in months, not years.
  • Selling makes it real. A fall on screen is a paper loss; it only becomes permanent if you sell into it.

Time in the market, not timing it

The market's best days often cluster right after its worst ones. Missing just a handful of them can erase years of returns — which is why staying invested is how a long-term plan does its work.

Calm by default
Next dip, he doesn't panic-check. He trusts the app to surface only what matters — and to stay quiet otherwise. The behaviour has stuck.
Habit: established
The guardrail that protects the habit: a hard cap of 2–5 nudges per week, counted across push, email and in-app. The habit is built on restraint — every nudge that fires has earned its place, so the investor never learns to ignore them.
