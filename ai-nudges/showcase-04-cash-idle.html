What inflation does to idle cash

Cash keeps its number, but not its buying power. When prices rise faster than the interest your cash earns, the same balance buys a little less each year — even though the figure on screen never drops.

What AED 750,000 really buys over time

In 5 years
≈ 660k
In 10 years
≈ 580k
If the balance earns ~0.5% while prices rise ~3% a year, the number stays put — but it buys roughly AED 170,000 less in ten years.

Why it's easy to miss

  • The number never falls. The erosion is invisible — your balance looks identical while it quietly buys less.
  • It compounds. A small yearly gap becomes a meaningful sum over several years.
  • Not all cash is "idle." An emergency buffer and near-term spending should stay liquid — it's the surplus sitting still that drags.

The trade-off to understand

Options that aim to outpace inflation usually ask for something in return — less instant access, or some ups and downs in value. Knowing how much cash you genuinely need on hand is the first step in deciding what the rest could do.

03

Habit Building

The habit here is cash awareness — noticing when money is drifting unproductively. Because it's educational and never pushy, trust builds from it being genuinely useful and rare, not from frequency.

Cash builds up
Quietly noticed
A large balance sits idle for weeks. Instead of a generic education push, the app waits and surfaces it in-app, tied to that real balance.
Trust: relevant, not spammy
First read
Learns the cost
The inflation-drag visual and the “AED 15,000 a year” figure make an abstract idea concrete — and the Learn note explains the why on demand.
Trust: taught, not sold
Acts or learns
Puts cash to work
He moves part into a deposit and keeps a buffer — or simply learns and waits. Either way the nudge proved useful without pressure.
Habit: starts to form
Over time
Cash-aware by default
Because it only fires when a real balance builds up, he learns to trust it — and starts thinking about idle cash before the app even asks.
Habit: established
The guardrail that protects the habit: education is off by default, never pushed, and only fires when pinned to a live event (a real idle balance) — all under the global 2–5 per week cap. Rarity is what keeps a sophisticated investor from switching it off.
