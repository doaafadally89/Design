Same agent, four front doors — compare the trade-offs.
The benchmark recommended a top-bar → docked-panel pattern as the Core. These five concepts isolate each entry mechanism — three summon the agent, two bring it to you — so we can test discoverability and fit before committing. Each opens the working dashboard with that entry auto-demonstrated.
✦Ask AI
A✓ Core — recommended

Top-bar button → right-docked panel

A labeled ✦ Ask AI button in the top bar opens a panel on the right that overlays the dashboard and persists across navigation.
Pros
  • Highest discoverability
  • Coexists with the data — side by side
  • Room for tables & citations
Cons
  • Eats horizontal space
  • Less "in context" alone
Pattern source: Power BI Copilot · Microsoft 365 · Adobe Experience Cloud

BStrong alt

Command palette (⌘K) — search becomes chat

The existing ⌘K search doubles as the AI entry: start typing a question and an Ask AI action appears, handing off into the panel. One omnibox for nav, search, and AI.
Pros
  • Keyboard-first, very fast
  • Reuses a control RMs know
  • Zero new chrome
Cons
  • Lower discoverability for non-power users
  • Hidden until invoked
Pattern source: Linear · Raycast · Vercel · omnibox search-to-chat
Open concept B
CPhase 2

Left-nav AI assistant → full-screen workspace

The existing AI assistant nav item opens a dedicated full-screen workspace with a conversation-history rail — for deep sessions and longer analysis.
Pros
  • Signals AI as a first-class place
  • Holds history & long sessions
  • Maximum focus & space
Cons
  • Pulls RM out of the dashboard
  • Out of data context
Pattern source: Microsoft 365 Copilot app · Adobe left-nav launcher
Open concept C
DNew idea

Proactive AI Insights widget

An ambient card embedded in the dashboard itself that proactively surfaces what changed in the book — drift, idle cash, news exposure — each with "Ask about this →" that opens the panel pre-loaded.
Pros
  • AI comes to you — no summoning
  • Surfaces value before the RM asks
  • On-ramp to Next Best Actions
Cons
  • Risk of notification fatigue
  • Takes dashboard space
Pattern source: Tableau Pulse · proactive insight digests
Open concept D
ENew idea

Start-of-day brief — auto-launch

On login, the panel opens itself with a proactive morning brief — overnight drift breaches, news touching the book, and idle cash — each item drilling straight into the data. No summoning; the day starts with the AI.
Pros
  • Zero discovery cost — it's just there
  • Frames the RM's whole day
  • Strongest "AI comes to you" story
Cons
  • Interrupts if RM had another intent
  • Needs a dismiss / "don't auto-open" pref
Pattern source: Morgan Stanley AI @ Morgan Stanley Debrief · Bloomberg morning run

Recommendation to validate

The benchmark points to A (top-bar → docked panel) as the production target for discoverability, with B (⌘K) as a power-user accelerator and C as a phase-2 deep-work destination. D (proactive insights) and E (start-of-day brief) are the differentiators — the "AI comes to you" story and on-ramp to Next Best Actions. These five let us put each in front of RMs before locking the approach.
