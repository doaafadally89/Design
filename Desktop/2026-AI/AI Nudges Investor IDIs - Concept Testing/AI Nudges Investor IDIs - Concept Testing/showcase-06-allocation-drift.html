What allocation drift is

Allocation drift is when market moves push your mix away from its target. A rally lifts your winners fastest, so their share grows — and your portfolio's risk quietly climbs without you doing anything.

How drift creeps up if left alone

In a rising market a 60% equity plan can drift toward 80% — taking on risk you never chose.

By the time a downturn arrives, the drifted portfolio is at its riskiest — so the fall hits hardest exactly when you can least afford it.

Why it matters over the long run

  • Risk rises on autopilot. You don't have to trade for your equity weight to grow — a rising market does it for you.
  • It peaks at the worst moment. Drift is largest after a long rally — right before the downturns it makes more painful.
  • Rebalancing is built-in discipline. Trimming what's run up and topping up what's lagged is a structured "sell high, buy low."

The point of rebalancing

It isn't about predicting the market — it's about keeping your portfolio the one you actually chose, so its risk stays matched to your plan.

What triggered it

Your equity allocation drifted more than 15% above its target weight.

What it used

Only your own holdings and the target allocation you set — nothing is shared outside the app.

How it's created

Our AI selects from a pre-approved library of nudges. It surfaces the factual gap but never gives investment advice — that's for a licensed advisor. Every nudge is logged.

Why it routes to an advisor

Rebalancing involves securities decisions, which require a licensed advisor — so this nudge hands off to a human rather than acting in-app.

You're in control

Mute or fine-tune risk reviews whenever you like.

03

Habit Building

Allocation drift is gradual and invisible day-to-day. The habit this builds is periodic awareness — trusting the app to catch drift early and loop in a human for the actual rebalance, rather than nagging or auto-trading.

It re-fires only if the drift persists, under the global 2–5 per week cap.
